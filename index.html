Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Nisab for Zakat in 2025?

Nisab is calculated using either gold (87.48 grams) or silver (612.36 grams) — whichever results in the lower value is used. Based on current Pakistani gold prices of approximately PKR 22,000/gram, the gold Nisab is around PKR 19,24,560. The silver Nisab at PKR 275/gram is approximately PKR 1,68,399. Most scholars use the silver standard as it allows more people to pay Zakat.

Is Zakat due on property and real estate?

Zakat is not due on your primary home or property used for personal use. However, if you own property for the purpose of selling it (investment property), then Zakat is due on its market value. Rental income is included as part of your zakatable wealth.

What is the difference between Ushr and Zakat?

Zakat applies to wealth (savings, gold, silver, stocks, business inventory) and is 2.5% of net wealth held for one full lunar year above the Nisab. Ushr applies specifically to agricultural produce and is paid at every harvest — 10% for rain-fed crops and 5% for irrigated crops. Ushr does not require a Hawl (one year holding period).

Can I pay Zakat in installments throughout the year?

Yes. While Zakat becomes due once a year (on the anniversary of your wealth reaching Nisab), you are permitted to pay it in monthly installments throughout the year as long as the full amount is paid by your Zakat due date.

Who is eligible to receive Zakat?

According to the Quran (9:60), Zakat must be given to: the poor (Fuqara), the needy (Masakin), those employed to collect Zakat, those whose hearts are to be reconciled, those in bondage, debtors, those striving in the cause of Allah, and travelers in need. It cannot be given to direct family members whom you are financially responsible for.
